Program

This performance is dedicated to you.

The seed of this piece began in the middle of winter, 2016. The world was starting to feel like a little too much. It emerged as a reaction to a perceived saturation in political, physical and emotional violence. I have seen a lot of artists react to this epoch by making work that reflects the brutality of the world - while this response makes sense to me and feels important, I personally struggle to receive these pieces when I feel I'm already maxed out. We gathered together to help make a performance piece that aims to be empowering and restorative - to offer an audience a chance to be soft in a hard world.

Good Things To Do began as a performance installation, an act of in-person care through a medium that often feels isolating. But as the pandemic had us looking for intimacy in unexpected ways, the jump to a fully digital iteration of this piece felt strangely natural. It has meant a lot to us to offer an experience that is restorative, that can leave others feeling more resilient and ready to face the world. It's important to stay awake and aware of the world around us. But if we don't use our muscles for recovery and resilience, they will atrophy. They will die.

This performance is dedicated to you, who can survive brutalities that are systemic or circumstantial, catastrophic or ongoing, death by a thousand cuts or one crushing blow. This is for you, who deserves a precious moment with yourself, whose gifts are essential to the world and must be kept available. This is our offering.

This is for you.

- Christine, Mishelle, Molly, & Sam

The Makers

Mishelle Cuttler – co-creator, sound design + composition

Mishelle is a Vancouver based composer and sound designer whose work is centred around the integration of sound, music, and storytelling. She has an MFA in Musical Theatre Composition from NYU’s Tisch School of the Arts, and a BFA in Acting from UBC.

Since gathering restrictions began in March of 2020, Mishelle has been fortunate to find multiple opportunities to make music safely - both through digital streaming work, and socially distant outdoor performances when possible. Some recent projects include The Quarantettes - a live and recorded project of poetry set to music and Made in Canada - an album, podcast and digital compendium with music written using the verbatim words of Latinx migrant farm workers. This summer Mishelle will be co-leading the music team for Break Horizons: A Concert Documentary by Kim Harvey as part of the Transform Festival at The Cultch, and designing sound for Done/Undone with Bard on the Beach. mishellecuttler.com.

Molly MacKinnon – co-creator, sound design + composition

Molly is a violinist and collaborative artist based on the unceded territories of the Coast Salish people. An interdisciplinary creator and performer with a passion for storytelling through music, Molly has worked with Vancouver New Music, the Vancouver Symphony Orchestra, Little Chamber Music, and many other groups around town. Recent collaborations include The Quarantettes;  a roving singing group brought into being during the pandemic, a composition for Vancouver New Music's One Page Score project, and Carousel Theatre's audioplay Rishi & d Douen, produced by Rumble Theatre. Molly is also the co-founder of Concerts On Tap, a series that pairs breweries and classical music. mollymackinnon.ca.

Christine Quintana – co-creator, text + concept

Christine is a playwright, actor, and theatre producer based on the unceded territories of the Musqueam, Squamish, and Tsleil-Waututh peoples. Recent writing/creation projects include: Someone Like You (Silver Commission audio play for Arts Club Theatre Company), Clean (translation and adaptation by Paula Zelaya Cervantes, audio explorations produced by Neworld Theatre and Aluna Theatre), The Fleetwing Fables (audio land walk for Caravan Farm Theatre), and Good Things To Do (immersive digital experience produced by Rumble Theatre).  Performance projects include Made In Canada (rice & beans theatre) and co-founding The Quarantettes, a roving singing group delivering neighbourhood tunes at a physical distance.  Christine is a graduate of UBC's BFA Acting Program, and a co-founding member of the Canadian Latinx Theatre Artist Coalition. christinequintana.ca.

Sam MacKinnon - collaborator, software design & development, promotional art

Sam is a web developer, designer, and animator. Pre-covid, he was the co-creater and host of the monthly variety show "At Least We Tried" at the Tipper in Vancouver. Recently Sam has worked on developing digital theatre solutions for Rumble Theatre (Vancouver) and Theatre Conspiracy (Toronto). He is always on the lookout for interesting creative collaborations! sam.w.mackinnon@gmail.com
