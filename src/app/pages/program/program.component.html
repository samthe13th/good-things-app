About Good Things To Do

This performance is dedicated to you.

I started creating this strange little creature in the middle of winter, 2016. The world was starting to feel a little much. It emerged as a reaction to the world's darkness, a perceived saturation in political, physical and emotional violence. I have seen a lot of artists react to this by making work that reflects the brutality of the world - while this response makes sense to me and feels important, I personally struggle to receive these pieces when I feel I'm already maxed out. I gathered some collaborators together to help make a piece that aims to be empowering and restorative - to offer an audience a chance to be soft in a hard world.

I didn't know what was to come when I started making this. Globally, in my community, personally. I didn't know I'd be constantly redefining what is 'survivable'. I spend a lot of my time as a human trying to alert myself and others to the brutality that permeates our lives. It's proven a lot more challenging to make something that is restorative, that can leave others feeling more resilient and ready to face the world. It's important to stay awake and aware. But if we don't use our muscles for recovery and resilience, they will atrophy. They will die.

This performance is dedicated to you, who can survive brutalities that are systemic or circumstantial, catastrophic or ongoing, death by a thousand cuts or one crushing blow. This is for you, who deserves a precious moment with yourself, whose gifts are essential to the world and must be kept available. This is our offering.

This is for you.

- Christine Quintana and the Good Things Collective

The Makers

Christine Quintana - creator

Christine is a playwright, actor, and co-artistic director of Delinquent Theatre (delinquenttheatre.com). She is a graduate of UBC's BFA Acting Program and the 2017 Siminovitch Protégée Prize winner for Playwriting. christinequintana.ca

Molly MacKinnon - collaborator, live music+concept

Molly is a freelance violinist and collaborative artist based out of Vancouver, BC. She is Artistic Associate of The Little Chamber Music Series That Could, an innovative music series that highlights new works by Vancouver artists with a focus on multi-arts pollination, community engagement, and local history. mollymackinnon.ca.

Sam MacKinnon - collaborator, digital design

Sam is a software engineer, musician, and animator. He currently works at Copperleaf Technologies as a UI/UX developer and plays drums for the band YAWN. He is always on the lookout for interesting creative collaborations.www.sammackinnon.ca

Mishelle Cuttler - collaborator, recorded sound

Mishelle is a composer, sound designer and musician. This summer she will be creating and performing music for Lysistrata at Bard on the Beach. She has an MFA in Musical Theatre Composition from NYU. mishellecuttler.com

Kaitlin Williams - collaborator, host

Kaitlin Williams is a theatre artist and works as an actor, director and designer. She previously worked with Christine as the costume designer for Delinquent Theatre's Jessie award winning production of Stationary: A Recession Era Musical. www.kaitlinwilliams.com.

Llowyn Ball - musician
